# Corporate Travel & Reimbursement Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025  
**Policy Number:** TRP-2025-001  
**Applies To:** All employees  
**Last Updated:** June 2025

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for business travel and reimbursement of travel-related expenses. All employees must follow these guidelines when traveling on company business.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and consultants traveling on behalf of the company. All travel must be pre-approved by the employee's direct manager.

## 3. Travel Authorization

- All business travel must be pre-approved before booking.
- Domestic travel requires manager approval.
- International travel requires director-level approval.
- Travel exceeding $15,000 requires VP / Finance approval.

## 4. Airfare / Flights

- Economy class is the standard for all domestic flights.
- Economy class is the standard for international flights under 6 hours.
- Business class is permitted for international flights over 6 hours with director pre-approval.
- First class is not reimbursable under any circumstances.
- Employees should book flights at least 14 days in advance when possible.
- Maximum reimbursable amounts:
  - Domestic economy: **$1,500** per round trip
  - International economy: **$5,000** per round trip
  - International business (pre-approved): **$8,000** per round trip

## 5. Hotel / Accommodation

- Hotel bookings should be standard rooms at mid-tier business hotels.
- Employees should choose hotels within company-negotiated rates when available.
- Maximum per-night rates (room + tax):
  - Domestic: **$200** per night
  - International: **$350** per night
- Extended stays (> 5 nights) may qualify for discounted corporate rates.
- Suites and luxury rooms are not reimbursable without VP pre-approval.

## 6. Meals & Per Diem

- Meal expenses are reimbursed based on actual receipts up to the daily limit.
- Daily meal limits:
  - Domestic: **$75** per day
  - International: **$100** per day
- Alcohol is **not** reimbursable, even during business dinners.
- Team dinners exceeding $50/person require manager pre-approval.
- Tips up to 20% are reimbursable and included in the daily limit.

## 7. Ground Transportation

- Taxi, rideshare (Uber/Lyft), and public transit are reimbursable.
- Daily ground transportation limit: **$100** per day.
- Rental cars require pre-approval and are limited to mid-size category.
- Parking fees are reimbursable up to **$40** per day.
- Tolls are fully reimbursable with receipts.

## 8. Incidentals

- Incidental expenses (Wi-Fi, tips, phone charges) are reimbursable up to **$50** per day.
- Laundry is reimbursable for trips exceeding 5 consecutive days.
- Personal items, entertainment, and minibar are **not** reimbursable.

## 9. Conference & Event Registration

- Conference registration fees are reimbursable up to **$2,000** with manager pre-approval.
- Registration above $2,000 requires director approval.

## 10. Receipt & Documentation Requirements

- **All expenses above $25** require an itemized receipt.
- Receipts must show vendor name, date, itemized charges, and total.
- Credit card statements alone are **not** sufficient — itemized receipts are required.
- Missing receipts for expenses above $25 will result in deduction of that expense.
- Digital receipts (photos, PDFs, email confirmations) are accepted.

## 11. Submission Deadlines

- Claims must be submitted within **30 days** of trip completion.
- Late submissions (31-60 days) require manager justification and may be subject to delayed processing.
- Claims submitted after 60 days will **not** be reimbursed.

## 12. Currency

- All claims should be submitted in **USD**.
- Foreign currency expenses will be converted at the exchange rate on the date of the expense.
- Employees traveling internationally should note the local currency and amounts on receipts.

## 13. Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following are **not** reimbursable:
- First-class airfare
- Luxury hotel suites (without VP approval)
- Alcohol
- Personal entertainment or activities
- Spa, gym, or wellness services
- Minibar charges
- Travel insurance (company provides coverage)
- Fines, penalties, or traffic tickets
- Expenses for non-business companions

## 14. Approval Matrix

| Total Claim Amount     | Required Approver         |
|------------------------|---------------------------|
| ≤ $500                 | Auto-approved             |
| $501 – $5,000          | Direct Manager            |
| $5,001 – $15,000       | Director                  |
| > $15,000              | VP / Finance              |

## 15. Fraud & Compliance

- Submitting false or inflated claims is grounds for disciplinary action.
- Duplicate submissions will be flagged and rejected.
- The company reserves the right to audit any reimbursement claim.
- Employees must cooperate with audits and provide additional documentation if requested.

## 16. Exceptions

- Policy exceptions require written approval from a VP or above.
- Exception requests must include business justification.
- Approved exceptions are valid for a single trip only.
